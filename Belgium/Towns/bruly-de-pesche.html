Brûly-de-Pesche



Brûly de Pesche is a town in Belgium, located in the Waloon province of Namur. It's a small isolated town within the large forest of the Belgian Thiérache and is next to a tributary of the L'eau noire river.

Previously a hamlet in the village of Pesche, it had become a municipality in its own right, by royal decree of King Leopold II , on 7 June 1867 until the 1977 merger of the municipalities. It covers 1,091 hectares, 935 of which are forests. Brûly-de-Pesche is also located on the territory of the Entre-Sambre-et-Meuse National Park, established in december 2022.
